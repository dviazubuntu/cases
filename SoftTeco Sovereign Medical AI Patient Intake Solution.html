New · The doctor's emotional model

It reads how a patient feels — and answers accordingly

Beyond the words, the agent models patient affect from voice prosody and language, then shapes its own tone, pacing and empathy. A frightened patient is met with reassurance; a confused one with patience.

  • 01

    Sense

    Prosody, pace and lexical cues estimate anxiety, distress, confusion and trust in real time.

  • 02

    Adapt

    The clinical persona shifts emotional coloring — warmth, calm, encouragement — to match the patient's state.

  • 03

    Pace

    A neural turn-taking model holds silence, avoids interrupting, and slows down when someone is struggling.

  • 04

    Escalate

    Sustained distress or risk language hands the session to a human clinician, with full context attached.

Under the hood

The voice pipeline

Five stages turn a spoken sentence into a structured, empathetic exchange — inside a strict sub-500 ms round-trip budget.

01

Detect

FireRedVAD

Endpoints speech and hands off clean audio chunks the moment a patient stops.

02

Transcribe

Whisper

Streaming ASR with Arabic-dialect tuning and word-level timestamps.

03

Reason + feel

Emotional LLM

Extracts medical entities and sets the emotional coloring of the reply.

04

Speak

KaniTTS

Low-latency, natural Arabic and English speech with matched prosody.

05

Take turns

NAMO turn detector

Neural turn-taking so the agent never talks over a hesitant patient.

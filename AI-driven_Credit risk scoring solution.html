Large open-weight reasoning models in the 300+ billion-parameter class, classical data-science scoring trained on the bank's own portfolio, and an agentic forensics layer that verifies every case before it is scored.

Domain-adapted document model, trained on the bank's data

On-prem

For structured extraction from bank statements, financial reports, bureau exports and ID documents, a general LLM is often overkill and slower than needed. We fine-tune a compact open-weight document model on the bank's own document types and historical cases. It runs cheaper per case and holds up measurably better on the bank's specific forms than a general model prompted ad hoc. When the highest scoring quality is required, this domain training on the bank's own data is the standard path, not an option.

Structured risk scoring: classic data science, done properly

CPU

Probability of default (PD), loss given default (LGD) and exposure at default (EAD) run on gradient-boosting ensembles trained and calibrated on the bank's own historical loan outcomes. Still the industry standard for tabular credit scoring: accurate, fast and natively explainable, unlike a pure LLM-based score. The full classical toolkit sits underneath: WoE binning with IV screening, a logistic-regression scorecard as an interpretable baseline and champion reference, stratified cross-validation plus strict out-of-time validation, and correlation/VIF pruning. Outputs map to expected loss (EL = PD × LGD × EAD), a master rating scale, and 12-month vs lifetime PD for IFRS 9 staging.

XGBoost / LightGBM / CatBoost PD / LGD / EAD component models Logistic-regression scorecard baseline (WoE / IV) Out-of-time and out-of-sample validation Isotonic / Platt PD calibration Monotonic constraints on key features SHAP + counterfactual explanations

Agentic forensics

On-prem

Before a case reaches scoring, autonomous verification agents run a forensic pass over the file: reconciling declared income against salary credits in the statements, cross-checking figures between financial statements, bureau data and the application, testing bank statements for tampering and synthetic-transaction patterns, and flagging fraud red flags (round-sum salary spikes before application, freshly opened accounts, mismatched employer details). Every finding is written into the case file with evidence, so the officer sees not just a score but what the agents checked and what they found.

Risk metrics

What the model produces, and how it is measured

Every case yields a standard set of credit-risk parameters, and every model version is judged against the validation metrics regulators and model risk teams actually use.

PD

Probability of default

Calibrated 12-month and lifetime PD, mapped to the bank's master rating scale and used for IFRS 9 stage allocation.

LGD

Loss given default

Expected loss severity given collateral type, valuation haircuts, seniority and historical recovery rates on comparable exposures.

EAD

Exposure at default

Outstanding balance plus expected drawdown of undrawn limits (CCF-based) for revolving and committed facilities.

EL

Expected loss and pricing

EL = PD × LGD × EAD per case, feeding risk-based pricing hints, cut-off decisions and portfolio expected-loss aggregation.

Explainable by design, governed like a regulated model

Credit scoring AI sits inside the bank's model risk management framework, not outside it. The platform is built around a human approval gate, not around automation for its own sake.

Human approval gate

The model produces a score and a recommendation; a credit officer or committee, determined by exposure and risk thresholds the bank configures in its own policy, makes the final decision. The system does not auto-approve or auto-decline outside rules the bank has explicitly defined and can audit.

Explainability and adverse-action support

Every score ships with SHAP-based top risk drivers in plain language, giving officers a documented basis for the decision and for any required adverse-action notice.

Champion-challenger retraining

New model versions are trained on newly labeled outcomes, then backtested against the current production model on a held-out sample (PD calibration, Gini/KS, PSI drift) and reviewed before a governance sign-off promotes them. The model never updates itself silently in production.

Fair-lending and bias testing

Disparate-impact and stability testing across relevant applicant segments runs as a standard gate before every model promotion.

Full audit trail

Every score, input snapshot, model version, policy rule applied and officer decision is logged, versioned and retrievable for internal audit and regulatory review.

Data residency and banking secrecy

On-premise hosting keeps applicant data inside the bank's own perimeter, supporting local data-protection and banking-secrecy requirements.

DISCOVERY2-3 weeks

DEV / UAT4-6 weeks

Build and model training on historical cases

Models are trained and validated on the bank's historical, labeled cases inside a development environment; officers test the console against known outcomes.

SHADOW MODE4-8 weeks

Parallel run, no live impact

The system scores real incoming cases alongside the existing process without influencing any decision, so accuracy and calibration are proven before go-live.

PRODUCTIONOngoing

Live decision support, under governance

The system enters live use once the bank's model risk function signs off; we operate it under SLA or hand it over to the bank's team with full runbooks.

Operating model

Infrastructure as code

Terraform and Helm charts for repeatable provisioning; container images are pulled once into the bank's private registry, then deployed with no further external calls. Air-gapped operation is supported.

Controlled rollout and updates

GitOps-style promotion (ArgoCD) moves signed, versioned releases through dev, UAT and production; every deployment is reviewable and reversible.

Backup and disaster recovery

Snapshotting and replication to a secondary on-prem node or DR site within the bank's own jurisdiction; RTO/RPO targets are agreed and configured per engagement.

Operate or hand over

We can run the platform under an SLA, or transfer full operation to the bank's infrastructure and MLOps team with documentation, runbooks and a defined knowledge-transfer period.
